MARKET SHARE

Collings Guitars is a privately held, founder-legacy boutique stringed-instrument manufacturer in Austin, TX. Founded by Bill Collings in 1973 in Houston and relocated to Austin in the mid-1980s, the company builds custom handmade acoustic guitars, electric guitars, archtops, mandolins, and a Waterloo-branded vintage-inspired sub-line. Bill Collings died July 14, 2017; the company continues under a leadership group including head luthier Steve Nall (joined 1999) and director of manufacturing Steve McNull. ~20,000 guitars produced since 1979 (NYT obituary); current 28,000-sq-ft facility employs nearly 100 (Texas Highways).

04Funnel Architecture▸

Acquisition: Earned media + organic social (Instagram-led) + artist visibility (top-tier players unpaid) + dealer-network co-marketing. No discoverable paid acquisition.

Conversion Path: Dealer-only. Discovery via social or artist context → collingsguitars.com → dealer locator (~70 US, ~14 international) → dealer holds inventory or takes custom order → 8–10 month lead → fulfillment via dealer.

Friction:

  • No DTC e-commerce; intentional scarcity but no buyer-journey telemetry
  • Long lead times (8–10 months) without visible nurture between order and ship
  • Email capture exists but no visible behavioral nurture flow
  • Limited owned-channel attribution because conversion happens at the dealer

Channels:

  • Dealer retail (~70 US, ~14 international markets)
  • CS email for sourcing only (customerservice@collingsguitars.com)
  • Free Friday factory tours in Austin
  • Trade shows (NAMM, Fretboard Summit)

  • Mandolin, archtop, ukulele/Waterloo lines under-merchandised vs. acoustics and electrics.
06Storytelling Diagnostic▸
07Opportunities▸

Succession & Steve Nall Story Arc

THE GAPThe post-Bill leadership story exists in a single substack interview; otherwise absent from owned channels. Heritage buyers care intensely and currently get no answer.
WHAT WE DELIVERDocumentary mini-series (3–5 episodes, owned channel) profiling Steve Nall, the wood-selection process, and post-2017 build-team continuity. Edited paid distribution on Instagram + YouTube.
Impact — Defends pricing power, neutralizes 'is Collings still Collings?' objections, refreshes the 2010 documentary for a new generation.

Hill Country / Q Spruce Launch Playbook

THE GAPHill Country (2024) and Q Spruce (2025) launches were leaked to dealer demos without a Collings-owned canonical video; brand attribution is dispersed and the message varies dealer-to-dealer.
WHAT WE DELIVERPer-launch flagship video + tightly-themed paid social burst + dealer co-op asset kit that ensures consistent messaging without losing dealer autonomy.
Impact — Compresses launch ramp, captures dealer demand into Collings-owned attribution, sets a repeatable playbook for C10 Traditional, forthcoming 00-size prewar prototypes, and any future Waterloo extension.

Price-Justification Narrative

THE GAPPer Steve Nall, Collings has raised prices significantly over five years and reinvested in employee compensation. The buyer has not been given this story — they only see the higher price.
WHAT WE DELIVERA hero piece on what a Collings actually costs to build (people, time, woods) plus a quarterly cadence of shorter 'anatomy of a build' clips. Positions Collings as the ethical choice in a thinning American luthier labor pool.
Impact — Protects gross margin during price increases; converts price-sensitive shoppers into mission-aligned buyers; deepens pricing power vs. peers.
